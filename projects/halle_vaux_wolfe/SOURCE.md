# Source

Feature geometry after:

Morris Halle, Bert Vaux & Andrew Wolfe (2000), "On Feature Spreading and the
Representation of Place of Articulation." *Linguistic Inquiry* 31(3): 387–444.

The `features.toml` encodes their structure (1): the root's dependents
([consonantal], [sonorant], [suction], [continuant], [strident], [lateral]), the
Place node over the oral articulators (Lips, Tongue Blade, Tongue Body), the Soft
Palate (velic) node, and the Guttural node over Tongue Root and Larynx. The
designated-articulator features ([labial], [coronal], [dorsal], [rhinal], [radical],
[glottal]) are privative (`unary`); the gradient properties are `binary`.

The lexicon and the single rule are illustrative, not from the paper: a minimal
nasal place-assimilation showcase for Place-node spreading. The letters cover only
the segments those words need.
